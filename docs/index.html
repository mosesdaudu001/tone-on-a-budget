Tone on a Budget — hear what CER misses

Yorùbá lexical tone in massively-multilingual TTS · Deep Learning Indaba 2026 (RIAD)

Yorùbá tone changes words, not just melody: ọkọ (husband) ≠ ọkọ̀ (vehicle) ≠ ọkọ́ (hoe). A synthesiser can be perfectly intelligible (low CER) yet say the wrong word by mis-tuning pitch. Listen for yourself.

ọkọ́ — “hoe” (High tone)

Carrier: Mo rí ọkọ́ ní ọjà — “I saw a hoe at the market.”

Why CER is blind to this

The same finetuned clip, with its pitch flattened (PSOLA). The words are unchanged — an ASR still transcribes it correctly, so CER barely moves — but the tone is destroyed, so the meaning is wrong. Our reference-free metric (tone_i2) catches exactly this; CER cannot.
